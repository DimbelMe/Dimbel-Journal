---
title: Dreadful Nightmares of A Sane Man
---
He's back. I knew it as soon as I saw the crime scene, as soon as I smelled the saltwater, as soon as I saw the pages, as soon as I saw the body.

Margaret, poor old Margaret, her life snuffed out, and her body... Oh, her body. In a million pieces, in a million and one. 

Her small apartment adorned by her blood, smeared on the walls and between pages, stapled to the walls and the pieces of what was once a human. 

The pages are the clue, they've always been, but they won't reveal themselves. I need time, which I lack. I seem to struggle with sleep, maybe after so many grotesque cases and so many lives taken, it's taken a toll on my mind. 

As a detective, I've had to shoot only a couple times. But I've seen lives destroyed, massacred, and grotesquely adorn the walls of their home due to this monster.

Some kind of cultist, is all we know. He strikes during the night, his victims are always older people who live alone, and he never leaves a print.

He must have training of some sort, and is clearly not right in the head. To do this to their bodies, you can not be sane. 

Poor old Margaret, sprinkled around, while those paged depict a ruined city.

And the blood, clearly a ritual. Circles of it all around, candles long-extinguished by the time we are called.

We know a few things. His MO, his approximate age, the fact that he has forensic knowledge of a higher level than normal civilians... And his obsession with me.

My name is plastered in every scene. Or hidden within the pages of delusion left behind. Sometimes it's a center piece of his insanity, other times just a foot note at the end of it. 

But I don't understand why. No prints, no face, no knowledge of his existence. It's a ghost that wants to send a message
