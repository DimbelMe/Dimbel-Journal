"There's nothing there, silly"
Mother would always say.
"It's just your imagination".

The old house's planks and doors, damaged by time, would wail in pain with every step taken inside. And after mother died, it got worse than I expected.
In the middle of the night, by myself in bed, I'd hear doors and floors creak and wail as if someone - or something - was walking around the old house.
I never got out of bed to check, I'd repeat to myself what I'd always hear my mother say.
"There's nothing there, silly", "it's just your imagination"
The creaking, the sounds of steps and a heavy breathing could be heard on the other side of my bedroom door. Always closed and locked, almost wishful thinking that it'd stop the nothingness outside from being more than that.
Today was a long day, and as I lay ro rest on bed I could hear the noises get louder.
Every step sound out in the hallway, followed by a slow creaking sound from the planks, followed by a heavy breathing noise that does not allow me to think, followed by my mother's voice: 
"There's something outside"
"Run."
