---
title: Nothingness
---
"There's nothing there, silly"

Mother would always say.

"It's just your imagination".

The old house's planks and doors, damaged by time, would wail in pain with every step taken inside. And after mother died, it would be worse and worse every night.

In the middle of the night, lying in bed, I could hear doors and floors creak as if someone - or something - was walking around the old house.

I never got out of bed to check, I'd repeat to myself what I'd always hear my mother say.

"There's nothing there, silly".

"it's just your imagination".

The creaking, the sounds of steps and a heavy breathing could be heard on the other side of my bedroom door. 

Always closed and locked, almost wishful thinking that it'd stop the nothingness outside from being more than that.

Today was a long day, and as I laid to rest on bed, I could hear the nothingness getting louder.

Every step out in the hallway, followed by the slow creaking sound from the planks.

Accompanied by the heavy breathing noises that didn't allow me to think, followed by my mother's voice: 

"There's something outside"

"Run."
