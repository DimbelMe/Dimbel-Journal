title: A Lost Conversation
---
This is new, isn't it?



What do you mean?



Well, all of this. The rain, the wind, the cold. It's all new.



New? We've had this in the world for as long as it has existed.



No, no. You misunderstood me. 



How could i have? You were very clear. It's new to you.



Again, you misunderstood me. It's not new to me, it's simply new.



And i already told you it isn't. 



Yes, but you have to understand. It's all new, this kind of cold, this kind of rain even this kind of wind. None of it shares the same essence as the one they had before.



I'm not sure i understand. The world doesn't change like that, nature doesn't change like that.



How can you be so sure?



I just am. There's no records, no memories, nothing. Nature has never changed by itself.



So it has always felt this way? Throughout thousands of years, the same cold, same wind, same rain?



Same cold, same wind, same rain. Always has been and forever will be.



That's really sad.



How come?



Well, we change so much, we learn and grow and the way we percieve this world of ours changes. But they will forever look outwards from the same spot. They will never see themselves as nothing more.



I guess that's really sad, at least the way you put it. But what if they are happy with how they are now? Maybe they simply don't see a need for change?



Maybe, yeah. Maybe they don't, that'd be nice. Maybe someday we might feel the same.



Someday.
